2015 Sand Camo Sportster - XL Iron 883

Why did I choose a Harley?

Read More

I was much more attracted to sport bikes like the Honda
CBR 600 or the Suzuki GSXR 600. My intention was to buy
a sportbike but my brother in law talked me out of it. He's
a Harley guy and convinced me to start out on something heavier
and not super fast. He knows I love speed and was looking out
for me I guess..

I didn't regret my decision AT ALL.
The sound of a Harley is incredible. Very loud and throaty.
Believe it or not, the loud exhaust saved my life several times.

Finished Motorcycle School

April 2015

I got my certification from the Motorcyle School in San Antonio. They're now permanently closed unfortunately.

Fenders are plast-dipped

May 2015

Plasti-Dip is a spray paint that you can peel off. It's kind of like a really thin rubber material and sprays on like a matte paint.

I wanted to keep the gas tank the original color but only focus on the fenders.

Install Vance & Hines Short Shot Exhaust

June 2015

I did a lot of research on what type of exhaust I wanted. I wanted to keep the black and sand camo combination and wanted a loud exhaust. I finally settled on the short shot exhaust.

Replaced air intake

June 2015

The better your motocycle can breathe, the more responsive it'll be. My factory air intake was great but not an open intake so I found a better alternative. This is a "naked" style air intake which means it's exposed. Sounds great whenever you turn it on, you can hear it sucking in air.

New helmet and motorcyle jacket

June 2015

Installed Vance & Hines Fuelpak

July 2015

The Fuelpak connects to the motorcycle's ECU which allows you to create fuel maps and tune your motorcycle. The coolest part about the Fuelpak is that it has bluetooth capability so you can see everything on your phone.

The Twisted Sisters Ride

July 2015

The twisted sisters is one of the best roads in Texas to ride. Incrdible turns, views, and amazing restaurants to visit. It was a total of 100 miles, very tiring to do on a sportster but it was worth it.

This route took us close to 6 hours to do, taking into account all of our stops

Riding in North Carolina

September 2015

In late 2015, I moved to Raleigh, North Carolina for a new job. I had the opportunity to ride all over the city of Raleigh, Durham and Cary. Much more biker friendly than San Antonio.
